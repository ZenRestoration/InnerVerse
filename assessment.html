Free Emotional Assessment

Discover your current emotional pattern.

This 2–3 minute check-in helps you name your current emotional pattern, dysregulation style, and nervous system tendencies — so you know exactly where to begin on the Mynd path.

Answer as you are right now, not how you think you “should” be. There are no wrong answers. This is a gentle mirror, not a test or diagnosis.

Optional details

You don’t need to add these to see your snapshot. They’re here if you want to save or share it later.

1. Right now, my body feels mostly…

Focus on your body first — not your thoughts or story.

2. Lately, I get thrown off most by…

Think about the last few weeks. What reliably knocks you out of balance?

3. In close relationships, I usually feel…

Go with the option that feels most familiar over time, not just this week.

4. When I’m overwhelmed, I usually cope by…

Write a few quick words. Don’t over-edit — your first examples are usually the clearest.

This is not a diagnosis. It’s a compassionate mirror of what your system might be trying to do for you. Use it as a starting point for curiosity, not a label you’re stuck with.

Next steps

Start with one small, doable thing. For most people, that’s the State lesson: learning how to notice “Oh, I’m in fight/flight/freeze/fawn” before trying to fix your whole life.

From there, you can move into Patterns, Needs, Boundaries, and Integration on the main Mynd path, at your own pace.

Quick legend: what these states are trying to do for you

FIGHT Protects you by pushing back — arguing, defending, controlling.
FLIGHT Protects you by moving faster — planning, fixing, avoiding stillness.
FREEZE Protects you by numbing — going blank, shutting down, “I don’t know.”
FAWN Protects you by pleasing — smoothing things over, caretaking, over-explaining.
