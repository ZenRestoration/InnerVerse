Lesson Two

The Narrator Within

Every human has an inner storyteller — part historian, part bodyguard. It wants to keep you safe, but sometimes it forgets you’ve grown.

Our thoughts are ancient protectors

The mind’s first job isn’t to make you happy — it’s to make you *safe*. Every belief, assumption, and judgment is an attempt to predict pain before it happens.

But protection can become imprisonment. Old stories — “I’m too much,” “I’m never chosen,” “I’ll fail again” — start to steer your perception long after they’ve expired.

Think of your stories like wallpaper

You stop noticing it after a while. It becomes “just how life looks.” But awareness lets you step back and realize — *this pattern was chosen by an old version of me.* You can peel it off gently.

Try this reflection

1. Think of a recent emotional reaction.
2. Ask: “What was I believing in that moment?”
3. Ask: “Is this belief still true now?”

That’s awareness. You just separated the *observer* from the *narrator* — and that’s how freedom begins.

Remember:

You are not your thoughts. You are the consciousness that hears them.
