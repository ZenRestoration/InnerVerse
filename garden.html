Your Attention is the Gardener

Story

Write a short, inviting story that illustrates attention shaping experience. (Example: noticing one kind thing changes the tone of a morning.)

Teaching

  • Attention selects reality. What we notice repeats.
  • Gentle focus beats force; curiosity sustains effort.
  • Rituals help you point attention on purpose.

Practice — “One Field, One Minute”

  1. Pick one small field (breath, sounds, hands on chest).
  2. Set a 60s timer. Stay with the field; gently return when mind wanders.
  3. Note one feeling word afterward. Repeat daily.
Tip: Anchor this to a daily moment (making tea, opening a door).

Reflection

  • Where does my attention go when I’m stressed?
  • What improves when I give something 60 seconds of care?

Mantra

“Where I look, I grow.”

Section • Soil — Belief

Belief is the Soil

Story

Short story where a kinder belief allowed growth.

Teaching

  • Beliefs are assumptions your nervous system predicts with.
  • Fertile soil = beliefs that are flexible and testable.

Practice — “Soil Test”

  1. Write a stubborn thought.
  2. Ask: 100% true? helpful? kind?
  3. Replace with a testable belief.

Reflection

  • Which belief feels compacted?
  • What experiment could loosen it?

Mantra

“I can learn new soil.”

Science Note

Brief on prediction error & cognitive reappraisal.

Section • Seed — Thought

Thoughts as Seeds

Story

Story: a repeated thought becomes a habit and result.

Teaching

  • Repeated thoughts wire attention.
  • Environment filters (mute/curate) protect the garden.

Practice — “Seed Tray”

  1. List 3 thoughts to plant.
  2. Write a daily cue for each.
  3. Review at night; water what you planted.

Reflection

  • Which thought sprouts fastest?

Mantra

“I plant on purpose.”

Science Note

Neuroplasticity basics; Hebbian learning.

Tip

Section • Water — Emotion

Emotions as Water

Story

Rain vs. flood metaphor; naming regulates intensity.

Teaching

  • Emotions carry needs.
  • Naming feelings reduces load (“name it to tame it”).

Practice — “Two-Minute Flow”

  1. Set 2 minutes; slow breath.
  2. Name: “I feel ___ about ___ because ___.”
  3. Ask: “What do I need?”

Reflection

  • Which emotions water you? Which flood?

Mantra

“Let it flow, not drown.”

Science Note

Affect labeling; vagal tone via slow exhale.
