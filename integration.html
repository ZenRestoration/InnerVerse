Pillar 05 — Integration

Integration: turning insight into a new normal.

Integration is where you stop collecting emotional concepts and start living them. It’s not a one-time breakthrough — it’s a daily relationship with your State, Patterns, Needs, and Boundaries.

Integration = repetition with kindness.

You will not “never get triggered again”. Integration means:

  • You notice sooner.
  • You speak to yourself more gently.
  • You choose slightly different actions, again and again.

Tiny shifts, repeated, become your new baseline.

You’re not behind — you’re in practice.
Every rep of self-responsibility rewires something.
Your nervous system learns through lived experience.

Daily practices: 5–15 minutes.

Here’s a simple menu you can pull from instead of doomscrolling.

Morning (2–5 minutes)
  • One State check-in (“what am I in?”)
  • One sentence about the Pattern you’re watching today
  • One Need you commit to honouring (“I will eat at least one real meal”)
Daytime (micro-moments)
  • 3 deep breaths before replying to hard messages
  • Standing up between tasks to feel your feet
  • One boundary sentence instead of over-explaining
Evening (5–10 minutes)
  • Journal: “When did my State change today?”
  • Note: “Which Pattern did I notice?”
  • Gratitude: “What did I do differently this time?”

Identity work: who you’re becoming.

Your nervous system loves familiarity. Integration asks you to become familiar with a new identity, slowly.

  • From “I’m too much / not enough” → “I’m a human learning new skills.”
  • From “I always fix everything” → “I’m someone who lets others hold responsibility.”
  • From “I never have needs” → “I’m someone whose needs matter, including to me.”
Identity affirmation: “I am someone who notices my state and responds with care. Even when I fall back into old patterns, I am still someone who is changing.”

Relationship upgrades.

Integration isn’t just internal — it shifts your relationships. Choose one relationship to gently upgrade over the next month.

  • Have one honest conversation using a need + boundary script.
  • Shorten or soften time with someone who consistently drains you.
  • Invite more support from someone safe (“Can I vent, or do you have capacity?”).
  • Celebrate one moment where you responded differently than before.

When old patterns return.

Regression is not proof that nothing changed — it’s proof that you’re human. Under stress, the oldest wiring often steps up first.

  • Notice: “Oh, I’m in an old loop right now.”
  • Normalize: “Of course this shows up here — this is a high-stakes area.”
  • Adjust: “What is one degree gentler or more boundaried than last time?”
Repair script (with yourself or others): “I slipped into an old pattern there. I’m noticing it now, and I’m choosing to respond differently going forward.”

Keeping the path alive.

You can cycle through these pillars as many times as you need. Life will keep offering new “labs” — new situations where your State, Patterns, Needs, and Boundaries get to practice.

When things feel overwhelming again, that doesn’t mean you failed. It means it’s time to revisit:

  • State: What am I in?
  • Patterns: What loop is trying to run?
  • Needs: What do I truly need?
  • Boundaries: What’s okay / not okay for me?

Integration is the spiral of your life — you pass the same themes from a higher, kinder vantage point each time.
