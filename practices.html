Practices

90-second practices that actually help

Gentle, body-first interventions for stress, panic, grief, numbness, and overwhelm. Choose one and do it for 60–120 seconds—notice your state shift.

Tip: softer jaw + slower exhales = instant safety signals to the nervous system.
Regulation Library

Start with breath, touch, or orientation

Physiological Sigh

Inhale, take a short top-up inhale, then long slow exhale through the mouth. Repeat 6–8×. This offloads CO₂ and softens sympathetic charge quickly.

4/6 Breath

Inhale for 4s, exhale for 6s for 1–2 minutes. The longer exhale cues safety and increases vagal tone. Keep shoulders low and jaw soft.

Havening Touch

Gently stroke the upper arms from shoulders to elbows while breathing slowly and saying, “I’m here with you.” Beautiful for shock and loneliness.

Ground Scan

Feet on the floor. Name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Orient to the room; return to now.

Shake + “Huh”

Shake arms and hands for ~20s; on each exhale a gentle “huh.” Completes stress cycles without aggression; resets tension loops.

Warm Hand

Hand to heart or cheek; soften tongue off the roof of your mouth. Whisper: “Safe enough to slow down.” Let that be true for a minute.

Mini-routines

  • Morning reset: 4/6 breath (90s) → sunlight at window (60s) → one glass of water.
  • After conflict: Shake + “huh” (30s) → slow walk (2 min) → name one boundary in one sentence.
  • Before sleep: Havening touch (60–120s) → write tomorrow’s one next step (1 line) → lights low.
