Practices

90-second practices that actually help

Gentle, body-first interventions for stress, panic, grief, numbness, and overwhelm. Choose one for 60–120 seconds and notice your state shift.

Softer jaw + longer exhale = immediate safety cues.
Regulation Library

Start with breath, touch, or orientation

Physiological Sigh

Inhale, top-up inhale, long exhale. Repeat 6–8×. Offloads CO₂; softens sympathetic charge.

4/6 Breath

Inhale 4s, exhale 6s for 1–2 minutes. Longer exhale cues safety (vagal tone).

Havening Touch

Gently stroke upper arms while breathing: “I’m here with you.” Beautiful for shock and loneliness.

Ground Scan

Feet down. 5 see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste. Orient to the room; return to now.

Shake + “Huh”

Shake arms 20s; exhale with a gentle “huh.” Completes stress cycles; resets tension loops.

Warm Hand

Hand to heart; soften jaw. Whisper: “Safe enough to slow down.” Let that be true.

Mini-routines

  • Morning: 4/6 breath (90s) → sunlight at window (60s) → one glass of water.
  • After conflict: Shake + “huh” (30s) → slow walk (2 min) → name one boundary in one sentence.
  • Before sleep: Havening touch (60–120s) → write tomorrow’s one step (1 line) → lights low.
