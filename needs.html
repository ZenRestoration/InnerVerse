Pillar 03 — Needs

Needs: the difference between soothing and truly being fed.

You don’t have “too many needs” — you have a nervous system that got used to going without. This lesson helps you understand what needs actually are, how they differ from coping, and how to communicate them clearly.

What is a need, really?

A need is something your system requires to function and feel safe enough over time. A coping mechanism is something you do to survive the absence of that need.

Examples of needs
  • To feel emotionally safe and not mocked for your feelings
  • To have predictable alone time to decompress
  • To be spoken to with respect, even in conflict
  • To know what’s expected of you (clarity)
  • To feel wanted, not just useful
Examples of coping
  • Shutting down instead of saying you’re hurt
  • Working nonstop to avoid feeling replaceable
  • People-pleasing so no one gets upset
  • Checking their phone every 5 minutes for reassurance
  • Using substances or distractions to not feel lonely

Needs create long-term nourishment. Coping creates short-term relief.

Emotional vs relational needs.

Toggle between the two and notice which list hits your body the most.

  • To have your emotions welcomed, not shut down or mocked
  • To feel like you’re “allowed to exist” even when you’re not productive
  • To have space to feel without being rushed to “get over it”
  • To experience joy and play, not just survival
  • To trust that someone will show up when they say they will
  • To be able to express concerns without being punished
  • To know where you stand in a relationship (no constant guessing)
  • To receive care, not just give it

Needs vs expectations.

Expectations are often unspoken rules we hold others to. Needs are truths about what our system requires to stay well.

Expectation

“You should text me back immediately or you don’t care.”

Underlying need

“I need to feel that I matter to you and that I’m not being ignored.”

Moving from “should” language to “need” language reduces shame and defensiveness on both sides.

How to communicate needs without apologizing for them.

Use this simple structure: context → feeling → need → request.

Script template:
“When <SITUATION> happens, I feel <EMOTION>. I realize I need <NEED>. Would you be willing to <SPECIFIC REQUEST>?”
Example:
“When plans change last minute, I feel anxious and unimportant. I realize I need more predictability. Would you be willing to give me as much notice as you can, even if it’s just a quick text?”

Identifying needs underneath coping.

Choose one coping behaviour and walk it through this mini-journal:

  • 1. Coping: What do I do when I’m overwhelmed? (scroll, work, drink, etc.)
  • 2. Relief: What feeling does it give me for a moment? (numb, in control, not alone)
  • 3. Need: What might I actually need? (rest, reassurance, being held, clarity)
  • 4. Micro-request: What is one small way I could ask for or give that need to myself?
