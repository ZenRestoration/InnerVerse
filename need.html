Lesson Three

Listening for What’s True

Every emotion is a messenger carrying a need. When we learn to listen, we evolve from reacting to responding.

Needs are sacred, not shameful

We were taught to hide needs to keep belonging. But needs aren’t demands — they’re directions. Safety, connection, dignity, truth, rest, play. When you meet the right need, the nervous system stands down.

Dashboard light analogy

A warning light means “check engine,” not “you’re a bad driver.” Emotions are lights on the dashboard of your life. You don’t smash the light; you tend the system.

Common pairings

  • Fear → safety, clarity, preparation
  • Sadness → comfort, witnessing, ritual
  • Anger → respect, autonomy, fairness
  • Shame → belonging, forgiveness, authenticity
  • Confusion → simplicity, guidance, time

Turn toward the body to learn the need

The body will answer if you ask kindly and wait. Place a hand where it hurts and ask, “What do you need from me right now?” Answers arrive as simple words or images.

Mini-practice: Ask & Answer

  • Hand on heart; soften jaw.
  • Ask the feeling, “What are you needing?”
  • Wait 10 breaths. Take the simplest action that honors it.

Your needs are not burdens. They’re the way your wisdom speaks.

Practice: One need, one step

Name the need in one word. Take one 5-minute step that serves it. That’s enough for today.
