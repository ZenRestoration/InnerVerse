Pillar 01 — State

Reading your state: what your nervous system is saying.

Before you can change anything, you have to see what you’re in. This lesson helps you notice fight, flight, freeze, and fawn, name your feelings, and offer your body small, realistic regulation tools — especially if you’re a sensitive, deep-feeling human who has carried a lot.

Nervous system 101 (the short version).

Your nervous system is not trying to ruin your life — it’s trying to keep you alive. It has different “gears” or states, depending on whether it senses safety, danger, or impossibility.

  • Safe enough: you can connect, think, play, rest.
  • Danger: you mobilize into fight or flight.
  • Too much / no way out: you drop into freeze or fawn.

Naming your state turns “what’s wrong with me?” into “oh, my body is trying to protect me right now.”

Nothing is wrong with you.
Your body has a history.
Your state is information, not identity.

The four protection states: fight, flight, freeze, fawn.

Tap each card to highlight the one that feels most familiar today.

State
Fight
Body: tight jaw, clenched fists, heat in chest/face.
Thoughts: “This is wrong. They’re wrong. I have to fix this now.”
Behaviour: arguing, defending, slamming doors, sharp texts.
State
Flight
Body: racing heart, shallow breath, can’t sit still.
Thoughts: “I have to do more. There isn’t enough time.”
Behaviour: overworking, over-planning, constant scrolling.
State
Freeze
Body: heavy limbs, numbness, foggy head.
Thoughts: “What’s the point? I can’t.”
Behaviour: zoning out, procrastinating, feeling detached.
State
Fawn
Body: tension in throat/chest, smile that feels glued on.
Thoughts: “If they’re okay, I’m okay.”
Behaviour: appeasing, over-explaining, self-abandoning.

Naming feelings: giving shape to what’s inside.

Your nervous system speaks in sensations; your mind speaks in stories. Emotional language bridges the two.

  • Body first: “My chest feels tight, my stomach is heavy.”
  • Then emotion: “I feel anxious, disappointed, and a bit angry.”
  • Then meaning: “Part of me is scared I’m not enough.”

Try this tiny script:

Right now my body feels <SENSATION>. Emotion-wise I’m noticing <FEELING WORDS>. It makes sense I feel this way because <REASON>.

Somatic basics: 60–90 second resets.

Regulation doesn’t have to be a 60-minute practice. Micro-movements done often are more realistic for a busy, layered life.

For fight (heat & anger)

Press your palms together and push for 5–10 seconds, then release with a slow exhale. Repeat 3 times. Imagine sending excess heat down your arms and out through your hands.

For flight (speed & panic)

Sit and place both feet flat on the floor. Look around and name 5 things you can see, 4 you can feel, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste.

For freeze / fawn (numb & collapse)

Gently tap along your arms and legs with your fingertips. Wiggle your toes. Whisper, “I’m here. I’m allowed to exist.” Start with movement that is 2/10 effort.

Example scripts: speaking from state, not shame.

Use language that honours your state instead of attacking yourself for it.

In conflict (fight/flight): “I notice my body is really activated right now. I care about this, but I need a short pause to settle so I don’t say things I’ll regret.”
When you’re shutting down (freeze): “I’m not checked out because I don’t care. I’m overwhelmed. I need some time to feel my body again and then I can respond.”
When you’re over-giving (fawn): “Part of me wants to say yes so you’re okay, but another part knows I don’t have capacity. I’m going to listen to that part.”

Your daily 3-minute State practice.

Use this as a simple, repeatable ritual — morning, midday, or before sleep.

  • 1 minute — Notice: “What state am I in? Fight, flight, freeze, fawn, or safe enough?”
  • 1 minute — Name: 2–3 body sensations + 2–3 feeling words.
  • 1 minute — Nurture: choose one micro practice from above that matches your state.
